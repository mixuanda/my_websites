## Three possible outcomes

After elimination, a linear system usually falls into one of three types:

1. one solution,
2. infinitely many solutions,
3. no solution.

The reduced matrix tells you which case you are in.

<Definition title="Solution-set types">
The reduced form of a linear system shows whether the system has one solution,
infinitely many solutions, or no solution.
</Definition>

## How to read the pattern

- If every variable has a pivot, the system has a single solution.
- If at least one variable is free and there is no contradiction row, the system
  has infinitely many solutions.
- If a contradiction row appears, the system has no solution.

<WorkedExample title="Recognize the three cases">
Consider these reduced matrices:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & | & 3 \\
0 & 1 & | & -2
\end{bmatrix}
$$

has one solution.

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & | & 5 \\
0 & 0 & | & 0
\end{bmatrix}
$$

has infinitely many solutions because one variable is free.

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & | & 3 \\
0 & 0 & | & 1
\end{bmatrix}
$$

has no solution because the last row is a contradiction.
</WorkedExample>

## Common mistake

<CommonMistake title="Free variable does not mean unfinished work">
A free variable means the solution set has a parameter. It does not mean the
problem is broken or incomplete.
</CommonMistake>

## Quick check

<QuickCheck prompt="What does `[0 0 0 | 1]` tell you about the system?">
Use the row to decide whether the system is consistent.
</QuickCheck>

<RevealSolution title="Answer">
It tells you the system is inconsistent, so there is no solution.
</RevealSolution>
