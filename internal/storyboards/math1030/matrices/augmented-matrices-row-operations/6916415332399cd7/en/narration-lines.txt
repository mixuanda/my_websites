[concept_intro]
Augmented matrices keep equation structure while making elimination systematic.
Every row operation must preserve solution equivalence.

[theorem_statement]
Elementary operations change representation, not the solution set.
That invariant is the reason Gaussian elimination is mathematically valid.

[example_walkthrough]
Name the target zero first, then select the row operation.
After each operation, read the new row as an equation to avoid blind manipulation.

[recap_checkpoints]
Most elimination errors are bookkeeping errors, not concept errors.
Consistent operation notation makes audit and QA possible.
