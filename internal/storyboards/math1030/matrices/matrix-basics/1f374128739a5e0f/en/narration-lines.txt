[concept_intro]
A matrix is a structured table, not a loose list of numbers.
Its dimensions tell us exactly how many equations and variables are being tracked.

[theorem_statement]
Matrix equality is strict: same shape and same entry in every position.
One mismatched entry is enough to invalidate equality.

[example_walkthrough]
Start by reading dimensions before touching any arithmetic.
Dimension mismatch immediately blocks equality checks.

[recap_checkpoints]
If you can read dimensions and entries confidently, elimination becomes much cleaner.
These checkpoints should be automatic before moving to row operations.
